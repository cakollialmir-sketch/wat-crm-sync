ADA Compliance Alert

Your Parking Lot Could Cost You
$75,000

The Department of Justice actively enforces ADA parking requirements. Property managers and business owners are personally liable — and a single complaint can trigger a full property audit.

The 4 most common violations in SC commercial lots:

  • Faded or missing wheelchair symbol markings
  • Missing or undersized access aisles beside ADA stalls
  • Insufficient number of accessible stalls per lot size
  • No van-accessible designation where required

Re-striping your ADA spaces costs less than 1% of a single violation fine.

Most commercial parking lots in South Carolina should be restriped every 1–2 years, depending on traffic volume and weather exposure. High-traffic retail lots may need annual service. ADA spaces should be inspected and refreshed at least annually to maintain compliance.

South Carolina follows federal ADAAG standards. Requirements vary by lot size: lots with 1–25 spaces need 1 accessible stall; 26–50 spaces need 2; and so on. Each stall must be at least 8 feet wide with a 5-foot access aisle. Van-accessible stalls require an 8-foot access aisle. We handle all calculations and markings to spec.
